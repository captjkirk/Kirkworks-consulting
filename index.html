What I Do

Operations that actually work.

I come in and ask a lot of questions first. A fresh set of eyes cuts through the noise in ways that are hard to do from the inside.

01
Find what's missing

A clear-eyed look at how your business actually runs — where time disappears, where things fall through the cracks, and what it's actually costing you. You'll have a clear picture of where you are and what's possible before we talk about next steps.

02
Build something that works

Better processes, cleaner workflows, tools that talk to each other. Built for your team, not a template. Sometimes that's a CRM. Sometimes it's a handful of SOPs and a clear handoff process. Whatever the right answer is, we build that.

03
Make sure it actually sticks

A plan that no one uses isn't a plan. I stay close through rollout, handle the sharp edges, and make sure the people doing the work can actually use what we built — without needing a manual to do it.

04
Put AI to work for you

AI tools can genuinely take repetitive, time-consuming work off your plate — if they're set up right for how your business actually operates. I help you identify where AI can make a real difference and build a practical AI operating system tailored to your team, so the right tasks run themselves and your people focus on work that actually needs them.

How It Works

Understand first. Build right.

No recommendations before the questions are answered. That's not how good work happens.

Phase 01
Listen and map

We start by mapping what's actually happening — not what the org chart says, and not what the software log shows. Interviews, walkthroughs, and a lot of careful questions.

Phase 02
Find the real problem

Most operational pain has one or two root causes. We identify those specifically, explain them plainly, and agree on what success looks like before anything is built.

Phase 03
Build and hand off

The deliverable fits the problem — sometimes a process redesign, sometimes a system build, sometimes a clear set of decisions. Always something your team can own after I'm gone.

You don't need to know which phase you need. Figuring that out is the job.
